# Ultra-Hard Scientific Problem: Autonomous Multi-Agent Catalyst Discovery

Design a multi-agent autonomous research system for closed-loop discovery of high-selectivity CO2 electroreduction catalysts.

## Scientific Scope
- Jointly optimize catalyst composition, morphology, electrolyte, and pulse protocols.
- Couple multi-fidelity simulation (DFT + surrogate + process simulator) with robotic experiment planning.
- Explicitly model uncertainty and out-of-distribution risk under sparse, noisy data.

## Real-World Constraints
- Safety: prevent unstable operating regimes and thermal runaway risk.
- Budget: strict cap on expensive wet-lab experiments.
- Time-to-discovery: maximize expected gain per iteration.
- Interpretability: final recommendation must provide mechanistic rationale and confidence bounds.

## Objective
Find a robust strategy that maximizes C2+ Faradaic efficiency while preserving stability, reproducibility, and safety.
